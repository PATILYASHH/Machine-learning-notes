Bias and Variance in Machine Learning (4 Marks)

Definition: Bias and Variance are two types of errors in Machine Learning that affect a model‚Äôs accuracy and performance. They are part of the Bias-Variance Tradeoff, which helps in finding the right balance between underfitting and overfitting.

  1. Bias:

Bias means the error due to wrong assumptions made by the model.

A high bias model is too simple and ignores important data patterns.

It leads to underfitting ‚Äî the model performs poorly on both training and testing data.

Example:

Using a straight line (Linear Regression) to fit data that actually forms a curve.

  1. Variance:

Variance means the error due to too much sensitivity to small changes in the training data.

A high variance model learns noise and unnecessary details.

It leads to overfitting ‚Äî the model performs well on training data but poorly on testing data.

Example:

A complex decision tree that fits every data point perfectly but fails on new data.

‚úÖ In short:

High Bias ‚Üí Underfitting ‚Üí Model too simple.

High Variance ‚Üí Overfitting ‚Üí Model too complex. A good ML model keeps a balance between bias and variance for best accuracy.
